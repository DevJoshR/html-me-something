The Climb Back

by Josh. R

If you have followed the nba within the last 5 years, then you most definitely remember just how dominant the Golden State Warriors were. They were a team that most fans either loved or hated. You may ask,"Why hated?" What exactly happened to the team that went on to break an all time nba record? Can they ever get back to being as dominant as they were years back?

This team lead by Stephen Curry(↓pictured below↓), in 2015, went on to win their first title in 40 years. As hinted previously, they went on to a 73 and 9 win/loss record the following year. While gaining much respect from fans. They showed that true selfless teamwork could accomplish a lot!

After a questionable loss in the 2016 finals, Golden State made a move that some loved and some really hated. They decided to take their 73 win team and trade for Kevin Durant, aka KD (↓pictured below↓). KD is a great player who some even consider to be the greatest scorer of all time.

After the acquisition of KD, this team was literally unstoppable. Demolished opponents effortlessly. Fans were crying everwhere about the unfairness....well not us Golden State fans..we loved every bit of it! Well. Thanks to our new acquired talent, the Golden State's historic run was followed by 2 more championship rings. It is also safe to say that many fans unfollowed the NBA because of the "unfairness." I also happen to personally know people who have quit the Nba because of it.

Well. just a few years ago, during their last great season, there was an altercation between Golden State's best player, KD, and Forward, Draymond Green. An altercation that was no more than two fueling egos, that would eventually cause issues to the team from within.

With the altercation being a factor in his decision to resign with the Warriors, KD decided he would take his great talents elsewhere. And being that Golden State had already traded a handful of great players to acquire him. KD leaving, left Golden State in a bad position. Which lead to a season so hard to watch. Lets just say, it was one of their worst seasons by a longshot. It was mindblowing watching a 73-9 win team fall to one of the worst teams to date.
